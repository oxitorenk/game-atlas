# 9 Kings Review

## Perfect

## Good
- I really enjoyed the core loop's mix of grid-based management and a rewarding three-card drafting system.
- The art style is visually engaging, and the SFX add a great sense of impact to each action.
- The quest mode is a standout, providing meaningful scenarios and a sense of purpose that keeps the gameplay from feeling aimless.

## Poor
- I found myself getting bored after about 30 minutes of play. 
- The game feels like a bit of a slow burn for my taste.
- A significant friction point is the lack of grid-buff previews—the game features target buffs that affect specific shapes (like diagonal grids), but it doesn't show you which cells will actually be boosted until after you commit, which makes tactical planning and grid management unnecessarily frustrating.
